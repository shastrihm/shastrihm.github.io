About

I am broadly interested in graphs and networks, including (but not limited to) graph algorithms, graph mining, machine learning on graphs, network science, distributed computing, combinatorial optimization, and applications thereof.

I graduated from Reed College in 2021 with a B.A. in Mathematics and Computer Science. There, I did my senior thesis with Profs. Jim Fix and Marcus Robinson, and an independent study with Prof. Eitan Frachtenberg.

I spent the Summer of 2020 at the Combinatorics, Algorithms, and AI for Real Problems REU (REU-CAAR) at the University of Maryland, College Park. There, I worked with Sam King and was advised by Profs. Andrew Childs and Alexey Gorshkov and Drs. Aniruddha Bapat and Eddie Schoute.

Since graduating, I have worked across the startup ecosystem in my hometown of Seattle, developing deep learning models for protein crystallography and developer tools for cloud-based data analytics. Wanting to return to research, I applied for and was awarded an NSF CISE Graduate Fellowship (CSGrad4US) that will generously support 3 years of my PhD.
